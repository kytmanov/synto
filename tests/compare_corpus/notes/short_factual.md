# Photosynthesis Basics

Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert light energy into chemical energy. Chlorophyll in leaves absorbs sunlight, primarily in the red and blue wavelengths. Water drawn from the roots splits into hydrogen and oxygen, and carbon dioxide enters through stomata on the leaf underside.

The overall reaction: 6 CO2 + 6 H2O → C6H12O6 + 6 O2.

The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes. They produce ATP and NADPH. The Calvin cycle then uses these carriers to fix carbon from CO2 into glucose in the stroma.

C3, C4, and CAM plants differ in how they manage water loss vs carbon capture. CAM plants like cacti open stomata only at night.

About 270 billion tons of oxygen are produced by photosynthesis globally each year.
